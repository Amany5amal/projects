Skills and tools required to enter the field of bioinformatics



You must be familiar with many sciences to enter the field of bioinformatics,
including: programming mainly,
  • basics of computer science

    • (it is important to learn to work on open source operating systems such as Linux),

  • biology

    • (biology in general, molecular biology),

  • information technology

    • (It is important to learn DBMS such as Oracle and MySQL).

    Many universities around the world offer programs to study this field at the undergraduate level,
    the duration of study is four years. If you hold a university degree in medical or biological sciences,
    or even in computer science, you can enroll in a two-year diploma in bioinformatics,
    or you can learn this field on your own by restoring what you lack from the four sciences mentioned above.




    Programming languages ​​to learn in bioinformatics


    Learning programming is a priority. If you are thinking of entering the field of bioinformatics,
    you have to master a number of programming languages ​​commonly used in this field, most notably:

  • Python language:

    • It is considered one of the easiest programming languages, simple,
    clear and widely used in the field of bioinformatics.

  • Perl

    • is widely used in building biological software.
    Not as simple and straightforward as Python, but it is a useful and flexible language.

  • R language:

    • a statistical programming language, ideal for graphing,
    that contains many free biological programming tools.
